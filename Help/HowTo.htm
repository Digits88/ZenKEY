ZenKEY Configuration - Quick guide


Here are some quick guides explaining how to perform some basic ZenKEY alterations

  1. Add a program or document.
  2. Create a menu containing other items
  3. Assign a Hotkey to launch a program, open a document or display a menu or fire an action.
  4. Select the right-click menu.
  5. Hide the ZenKEY window .
  6. Record a series of keystrokes to simulate

NOTE: All of these guides apply to the ZenKEY Configuration program. Changes only take place once 'Apply' is clicked.

1. How to add a program or document

  1. Click on the 'Items' icon on the left.
  2. In the ZenKEY menu tree, select the item before which you wish to insert your action.
  3. Click on the 'New' button on the right.
  4. Enter the details of the action you wish to create and click 'Done'.(See Adding and Editing Items for more details)

2. Create a menu

  1. Click on the 'Items' icon on the left.
  2. In the ZenKEY menu tree, select the item before which you wish to insert your menu
  3. Click on the 'New' button on the right.
  4. Enter details for the menu, making sure you select 'ZenKEY Menu' for the Action type' (See Adding and Editing Items for more details).
  5. Click on 'Done'

3. Assign a Hotkey to launch a program, open a document or display a menu or fire an action.

  1. Click on the 'Items' icon on the left.
  2. In the ZenKEY menu tree, select the item for which you wish to assign a Hotkey.
  3. Click on 'Edit' on the right.
  4. Click inside the 'Set' box and press the keys you wish to use.
  5. Click done

4. Select the right-click menu.

  1. Click on the 'Items' icon on the left.
  2. In the ZenKEY menu tree, select the menu you wish to see when you right-click.
  3. Select the 'Right click menu' checkbox on the right.

5. Hide the ZenKEY window .

  1. Click on the 'Setting' icon on the left.
  2. Uncheck the 'Always show form' checkbox under the 'Behavior' group on the top right.

5. Record a series of keystrokes to simulate.

  1. Click on the 'Items' icon on the left.
  2. In the ZenKEY menu tree, select the item before which you wish to insert your action.
  3. Click on the 'New' button on the right.
  4. In the 'Action type' drop down box, select 'Simulate a series of keypresses'.
  5. Click on 'Edit Keystrokes'
  6. 'Click on 'Record >>" and then 'Start recording'
  7. Press they exact keys that you want to simulate in the exact order that they should be pressed. You can you they buttons on the right to insert a pause (for the computer to do some processing...) or simulate keys that windows might react to (e.g. the 'Windows' key or 'Control+Alt+Delete)
  8. Click on 'Stop recording'.
  9. Click 'Done', 'Done' and 'Apply'
